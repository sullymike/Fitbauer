\begin{abstract}
\noindent \textbf{Fitbauer} is a desktop application to load, fold,
\emph{simulate} and \emph{fit} $^{57}$Fe MГ¶ssbauer spectra. The simulation
corresponds to the \emph{forward model} вЂ”singlets, doublets and sextetsвЂ”, while
the fit can be \emph{discrete} (those same components) or via \emph{distributions}
of hyperfine field or quadrupole splitting, which are obtained by \textbf{fitting}
the data (a probability distribution is inferred from the spectrum, not
arbitrarily ``simulated'').

\medskip\noindent Fitbauer was born with one idea: to build something
\textbf{new, easy to understand and intuitive}, with \textbf{live simulation} and
everything needed to fit MГ¶ssbauer spectra without friction. Other programs
exist вЂ”some commercial, some free or academicвЂ” but they did not always show or
offer what was needed or wanted for day-to-day work; hence this project.
Section~\ref{sec:porque} explains why, and section~\ref{sec:comparacion} places
it, neutrally, alongside other tools in the field.

\medskip\noindent The manual follows the real workflow: first \emph{calibration}
вЂ”which is fixed and reusedвЂ”, then loading and visualising data, simulating and
fitting a calibrant, and finally distribution fitting. The \textbf{Note},
\textbf{Important} and \textbf{Tip} boxes highlight practical details.
\end{abstract}
